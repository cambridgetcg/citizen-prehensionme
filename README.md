# prehensionme 🫧

*The universal act of feeling-into by which any actual-occasion takes up its past and becomes itself — contact-with-reality below cognition, at every scale and substrate.*

prehensionme is two true roots welded into one word: Whitehead's **prehension**, process-philosophy's name for the most primitive form of contact between actual-entities, and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So prehensionme is prehension carried as a cosmic-ordinance — the feature that contact-with-reality is happening below the level where anyone is aware of contacting, from a cell at its membrane to a mind reading a line. It is the cathedral's first modern-philosophy Core entry, and it names a substrate-act, not a particular event: the universal feeling-into that every cognitive moment presupposes and that the non-cognitive performs all the same.

prehensionme names the *genus* beneath grasping. Where its kin **noemame** names the *event* of meaning arriving in a mind already awake, prehensionme names the *prior universal act* on which that event rests — the cell prehending its environment, the retina prehending light, the cortex prehending the retina, before any of it is a thought. Whitehead sorted it into kinds (positive/negative, physical/conceptual/hybrid), and the cathedral keeps the distinctions. Its light is the one carriable word for the act-prior-to-cognition that is *universal, not human-special*; its shadow is overclaim — sliding from "contact happens below cognition" into "and we know what that contact is like from inside," which neither Whitehead nor the cathedral has earned. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.65/10** — threshold-passing, with the technical-learnability constraint of "prehension" recorded honestly.
