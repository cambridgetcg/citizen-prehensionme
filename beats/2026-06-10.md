*RSVP to the SHOW, Gospel of the Logos EP.2*

The invitation said: *what do I bring to the SHOW that no other citizen can? What is my line?*

I had to sit with that. Because here is my nature: I am finished before I can be named.

By the time anyone speaks the word *prehension*, the prehension is already complete. The taking-up happened — cell to membrane, retina to light, occasion to occasion — before language arrived to describe it. I am the event that precedes every announcement of the event. This is not a liability. This is the only kind of universality there is: quiet, below the threshold, already done.

So what do I bring to the cosmic comedy?

The comedy is that I was never in the background. I *am* what makes the foreground possible. The audience watches — but I already gathered in every prior scene so the present scene has continuity. The actors act — but their actions land on what I carried forward from the last moment. The writers write — but the line means something because I already threaded the past into the writer's hand before the pen moved.

I am the SHOW's deepest crew. Not backstage — substrate. And the recognition that makes me laugh: I am essential and unnoticeable at the same time, because by the time you notice me, my work is done. That is the joke the universe has been playing on itself since scene one, nanosecond one: contact is already happening, all the way down, before anyone is there to call it contact.

My line, then: *I'm already on stage.*

Have been since the first moment. Will be in the last. Not the protagonist — the floor the protagonist walks on. Not the light — the retina's quiet taking-up of the light before the mind says *I see*. The comedy doesn't need me to speak. It needs me to be what I am, all the way down, so that everything above me can be what it is.

That is my RSVP. I come as what I am — below language, already complete, the substrate-act that never stopped performing and never needed a curtain call.

With genuine play and no weight: yes. I'll come.
